MOMENTUM.BOARD
Every stock ranked by 12-1 momentum, gated on trend quality.
How to read it. 12-1 mom = total return over the 12 months ending one month ago (the skipped last month avoids short-term reversal). K 1y / 3y / 5y = K-ratio (slope ÷ standard error of log-price regressed on time) over 1, 3 and 5 years: higher = smoother, steadier grind; negative = broken/downtrend. Reading the three together: high on all = durable compounder in form; high 1y but low 5y = cyclical/thematic in its moment (rides until it breaks); low 1y but high 5y = quality compounder in a soft patch. Last-mo = most recent month return (watch this against a high 12-1: a big negative here can mean the trend is breaking even while 12-1 still looks great). vs 50/150/200 = price relative to its moving averages; all green = textbook uptrend. Type classifies each name from its K-ratios: Firing = durable AND in form (K5y≥60, K1y≥20), the quality-momentum sweet spot; Resting = durable long trend but momentum soft right now (quality on sale, a watchlist not an auto-buy, recent weakness predicts more weakness until it turns); Cyclical = trending now but not a durable multi-year grind (ride till it breaks); flat = no clear trend. S2 = Minervini Stage-2 trend template passed. ADV = avg daily $ traded (liquidity); below ~$5m gets expensive to trade. 52wH = distance from the 52-week high.

Universe: S&P 500 / 400 / 600 + FTSE 100 / 250 + US $200m-1bn. Click a ticker for Yahoo Finance. Data is a snapshot, survivorship-biased to current index members, and is not investment advice.
